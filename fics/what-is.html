What is the AEOI

The Automatic Exchange of Information (AEOI) is a process whereby tax information on residents who have accounts abroad is systematically and periodically transmitted to the jurisdiction of tax residence. Under these exchanges, the CRA sends information to partner jurisdictions and simultaneously receives information regarding Canadian residents who hold financial accounts offshore or abroad. The goal is to ensure tax transparency between countries and to combat tax evasion.

While AEOI can cover various categories of income, including dividends, interest, royalties, pensions etc.,  the focus of this webpage will be on the automatic exchange of financial account information.

Canada participates in two automatic exchanges of financial account information:

  • Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA)
  • Common Reporting Standard (CRS)

Both FATCA and CRS require financial institutions to identify customers who directly or indirectly hold financial accounts in jurisdictions where they are not tax residents. Financial institutions are required to ask customers to disclose their tax status and to submit documentation to support it. 

What is the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA)

The Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA) was enacted by the U.S. government in March 2010. It is a law that requires non-U.S. financial institutions to report to the U.S. Internal Revenue Services (IRS) any financial accounts held or controlled by specified U.S. persons which include U.S. citizens or residents. 

Recognising the legal and cost issues of this approach and the importance of applying the underlying policy goal of FATCA on a reciprocal basis to improve tax compliance the United States developed a model for the intergovernmental implementation of FATCA. The Model provides for the implementation of FATCA through reporting by financial institutions to their local tax authorities, which then exchange the information on an automatic basis with the U.S. tax authority.

The automatic exchange of financial account information with the United States is governed by an Intergovernmental Agreement (IGA) signed by Canada in 2014. The IGA was implemented through changes to PartXVIII, Enhanced International Information Reporting, in the Income Tax Act, which set out detailed requirements for Canadian financial institutions.

Under these exchanges, the CRA simultaneously receives similar information from the IRS regarding Canadians who hold financial accounts in the United States.

What is the Common Reporting Standard (CRS)

Developed by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) to combat cross-border tax evasion, the Common Reporting Standard is an international standard for the collection, reporting and exchange of financial account information. It requires financial institutions to report to their tax administration any accounts held or controlled by non-residents. Information is then exchanged with the jurisdiction where the account holder is resident.

The CRS was implemented in Canada on December 15, 2016 under Part XIX of the ITA and became effective on July 1, 2017. It requires Canadian financial institutions to report account information to the CRA who in turn exchange this information with partner jurisdictions under the provisions and safeguards of the Multilateral Convention on Mutual Administrative Assistance in Tax Matters or the relevant bilateral tax treaty. To date, more than 100 jurisdictions have signed on to the CRS.
