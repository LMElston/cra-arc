Who has reporting obligations

An entity has a potential reporting obligation in Canada, if it is a reporting Canadian financial institution for the purposes of Part XVIII (FATCA) or Part XIX (CRS).  A reporting Canadian financial institution may include:

  • Banks
  • Credit unions
  • Specified life insurance companies
  • Private equity funds
  • Managed funds
  • Exchange traded funds
  • Brokers

To be a reporting Canadian financial institution an entity must be all of the following:

  • Located in Canada
  • A financial institution
  • A listed financial institution
  • Not exempted from reporting (classified as a non-reporting financial institution)

An entity located in Canada means an entity resident in Canada excluding their branches located outside Canada and branches located in Canada of entities not resident in Canada.

An entity is a financial institution if it is:

  • A depository institution
  • A custodial institution
  • An investment entity
  • A specified insurance company

For a complete list of listed financial institutions and non-reporting FI refer to:

  • Guidance on Canada-U.S. Enhanced Tax Information Exchange Agreement - Part XVIII
  • Guidance on the Common Reporting Standard - Part XIX

The CRS has fewer non-reporting financial institution exemptions than FATCA.  Ensure you check the definition for both as it is possible that financial institutions that are not considered reporting financial institutions (RFIs) under FATCA are in scope for the CRS.

What does a reporting Canadian financial institution need to do

Canadian financial institutions, subject to Part XVIII and XIX, need to:

  1. Register with the IRS to obtain a Global Intermediary Identification Number (GIIN) for the purposes of Part XVIII
  2. Implement customer due diligence, including obtaining self-certifications and/or documentary evidence, to review and identify reportable accounts.
  3. Report customer accounts held or controlled by US citizens/US tax residents or other non-resident account holders that are reportable under Part XVIII and/or Part XIX.
  4. File an annual Part XVIII and/or Part XIX Information return with the CRA.
