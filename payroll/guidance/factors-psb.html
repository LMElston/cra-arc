Personal services business

Content has been developed to provide guidance only. The guidance does not replace the law and does not replace a formal request for a ruling. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

You need to consider multiple factors to determine if the worker is a personal services business (PSB).

A PSB is a business that a corporation carries on to provide services to another entity (such as a person or partnership) that an officer or employee of that entity would usually perform. Instead, an individual performs the services on behalf of their own corporation. That individual is called an incorporated employee.

Conditions to be considered a PSB

The worker is considered a PSB if all of the following conditions apply:

  • The incorporated employee who is performing the services, or any person related to the incorporated employee, is a specified shareholder of the corporation (a specified shareholder is an individual who directly or indirectly owns at least 10% of the issued shares of any class of a capital stock of the corporation or a related corporation)
  • The incorporated employee, if it were not for the existence of the corporation, would reasonably be considered an officer or employee of the entity receiving the services
  • The corporation does not employ more than five full-time employees throughout the year
  • The corporation's income is from services performed by the worker, as the incorporated employee, on behalf of the corporation
Example of a PSB

John Smith is offered a 20 month IT contract with ABC Corp. However, ABC Corp. requires John to offer his services through a corporation. John sets up a corporation for which he will be the sole employee and shareholder of that corporation. ABC Corp. then pays the newly set up corporation as John performs services for them. It will be John's responsibility to pay himself through his own corporation as he is an incorporated employee.

By comparison, Cliff Jones operates his own sole proprietorship and provides IT consulting and support to numerous clients. He controls his hours of work providing that the client's needs are met, provides the equipment and supplies, hires employees to help him do the work. If Cliff incorporates, and continues to have multiple clients and possibly employees, he is considered to be operating a business as a corporation.

This is in contrast to John's situation, where John only provides IT services to ABC Corp. His services would normally be provided by an employee of ABC Corp. Therefore, John's corporation is a PSB.

Definitions

What is a person

A trust, individual, corporation, partnership or a succession, or a body that is an association, club, commission, union or another organization of any kind.

What is a specified shareholder

A specified shareholder of the corporation is an individual who directly or indirectly owns at least 10% of the issued shares of any class of a capital stock of the corporation or a related corporation.

What is a full time employee

An employee who works a full business day on each working day of the year, subject to normal absences due to illness or vacation.

What is an associated corporation

A corporation is associated with another corporation in a taxation year if, at some point in the year if one of the following apply:

  • One of the corporations directly or indirectly controls, in any manner whatsoever, the other corporation
  • The two corporations are directly or indirectly controlled, in any manner whatsoever, by the same person or same group of persons
  • Each of the corporations is directly or indirectly controlled, in any manner whatsoever, by a person, and the person that controls one of the corporations is related to the person that controls the other corporation, and either of the persons owns at least 25% of the issued shares of any class, other than a specified class, of the capital stock of each corporation
  • One of the corporations is directly or indirectly controlled, in any manner whatsoever, by a person that is related to each member of a group of persons that controls the other corporation, and this person owns at least 25% of the issued shares of any class, other than a specified class, of the capital stock of the other corporation
  • Each of the corporations is directly or indirectly controlled, in any manner whatsoever, by a related group and each member of one of the related groups is related to all of the members of the other related group, and one person who is a member of both related groups owned alone, or several persons who are members of both related groups owned together, in respect of each corporation, at least 25% of the issued shares of any class, other than a specified class, of the capital stock thereof

Income tax obligations

For a PSB, the income tax obligations are different than other corporations.

Tax deductions and tax rates

A PSB is subject to the full federal and provincial corporate tax rates on all taxable income, plus an additional 5%.

A PSB is not eligible for:

  • General tax rate reduction
  • Small business deduction
Eligible expenses

A PSB is limited to all of the following deduction expenses:

  • Salary and wages the corporation pays to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Any benefit or allowance the corporation provides to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Certain expenses of the corporation associated with selling property or negotiating contracts
  • Legal expenses incurred by the corporation in collecting amounts owing

Filing and payment obligations

  • File a T2 Corporation Income Tax Return for the PSB (not eligible to claim the same tax deductions and expenses)
  • Remit (pay) the income tax (same as regular Corporations)
GST/HST obligations

For a PSB, the GST/HST obligations are the same as other corporations.

Withholding, filing and payment obligations

Withhold, file and remit the GST/HST (same as regular Corporations)

Payroll obligations

  • Payer

    • Does not deduct income tax, CPP contributions or EI premiums from the PSB (worker).
    • Fill out a T4A slip (or T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 slip depending on the situation of the PSB).

    Learn more: Payments of fees for services.

  • PSB

    • Fill out T4 slips for any employees of their corporation (same as regular corporations)

Result: You are not sure if the worker is a PSB or not

A corporation is carrying on a PSB if the incorporated employee that provides services to the person on behalf of the corporation could reasonably be considered an employee of that person if the corporation did not exist.

An employee-employer relationship between the incorporated employee and the person that received the services must be shown. Both are therefore bound by a contract of employment instead of a contract of enterprise.

The factors used to determine whether an employee-employer relationship exists are the same factors used to determine a worker's status as an employee or self-employed worker.

How to determine the employee-employer relationship

To determine the employee-employer relationship, continue to Determine the factors to consider.

If you determine that the worker is considered an employee, the worker is also considered a PSB.
