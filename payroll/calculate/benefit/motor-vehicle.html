Motor vehicle provided by the employer

You may be looking for:

  • Automobile or motor vehicle benefits – Allowances or reimbursements provided to an employee for the use of their own vehicle

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a motor vehicle to your employee or a shareholder, to use in performing their duties of employment. Generally, the personal use of the employer provided motor vehicle is a taxable benefit.

  • Employer
    Includes a person related to the employer
  • Employee and shareholder
    Includes a person related to the employee or shareholder
  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if a motor vehicle is an automobile

    The Income Tax Act defines motor vehicles and automobiles differently for tax purposes.

    Answer a few questions to determine if the vehicle is a motor vehicle or an automobile.

    Launch vehicle validator

    vehicle validator

    Automobile and motor vehicle definitions
    • If the vehicle is an automobile, do not continue to next step. Refer to Automobile provided by the employer.
    • If the vehicle is a motor vehicle, continue to Step 2 - Determine if you made the motor vehicle available to the employee.

  2. Determine if you made the motor vehicle available to the employee

    • If you did not make the motor vehicle available to your employee, this is not a taxable benefit. Do not continue to next step.
    • If you made the motor vehicle available to your employee, continue to Step 3 - Determine if there was personal driving of the motor vehicle.

  3. Determine if there was personal driving of the motor vehicle

    • If there is no personal driving, the use of the motor vehicle is not a taxable benefit. Do not continue to next step.
    • If there is personal driving, the use of the motor vehicle may be a taxable benefit. Continue to Step 4 - Determine if special considerations apply to the motor vehicle benefit.

  4. Determine if special considerations apply to the motor vehicle benefit

    • If the the use of the motor vehicle is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the use of the motor vehicle is not in one of the above special situation, the use of the motor vehicle may be a taxable benefit. Continue to Step 5 - Determine if the motor vehicle benefit is taxable.

  5. Determine if the motor vehicle benefit is taxable

    You need to calculate the value of the following:

    • Benefit related to the leasing of the motor vehicle
    • Motor vehicle operating expense  benefit when you pay the operating cost of the motor vehicle

    Continue to Step 6 - Review the required information (logbook) your employee must have kept.

  6. Review the required information (logbook) your employee must have kept

    • If the employee did not keep the required records or you did not receive a copy, you must calculate the full allowance or reimbursement you made. Continue to Step 9 - Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    • If the employee kept the required records and you received a copy, the benefit meets the condition 1 of X for the reduced rate calculation. Continue to Step 7 - Determine if the employee is required to use the vehicle in connection with or in the course of an office or employment.

  7. Determine if the employee is required to use the vehicle in connection with or in the course of an office or employment

    You need to determine if you can use the reduced rate calculation or the reasonable allowances rates calculation to calculate the employee's use of the vehicle between home and work. You can use the reduced rate calculation if all of the following conditions apply:

    • The vehicle is not an automobile
    • You tell your employee in writing that they cannot make any personal use of the vehicle, other than travelling between work and home
    • Your employee kept the required records of business and personal use
    • You have valid business reasons for making the employee take the vehicle home at night, for example:
      • it would not be safe to leave tools and equipment at your premises or at a worksite overnight
      • your employee is on call to respond to emergencies (either to the health and safety of the general population or to a significant disruption to the employer's operations), and you provide the vehicle so the employee can respond more effectively to emergencies
    • The motor vehicle is specifically designed or suited for your business or trade and is essential for the performance of your employee's duties
    Examples

    Example 1

    ABC Restaurant Inc. uses a van that has been specially modified so it can be used more efficiently to transport and deliver food orders. The employer has asked the manager, Leslie, to take the van home at night in case she needs to respond to an emergency at the restaurant, such as an after hour alarm or to fill in on busy nights. Leslie is not allowed to use the van for any other personal use. Since she is the manager, her employment duties do not include delivering food orders, even on a busy night.

    ABC Restaurant cannot use the lower (33 cents per kilometre) rate when calculating Leslie's taxable benefit. These circumstances do not meet all of the conditions of the administrative policy. Although the vehicle has been modified for business needs, Leslie does not need the van to perform her employment duties, so she does not have to bring it home for valid business reasons.

    Example 2

    Mary works for a gas utility company and has to be on-call two weekends out of every month. On these weekends, she has to be able to respond to emergencies directly from her home. The van, which the employer provides Mary for these weekends, displays the company's name, has a light on the roof and has been permanently modified to carry specialized equipment to the scene of the emergencies. The company has a written policy prohibiting any other personal use of these vehicles, other than driving between the worksite and home. Mary has not used the vehicle for any other personal driving.

    Since this situation meets all of the conditions of the administrative policy, Mary's employer may calculate the personal use of the vehicle using the reduced rate of 33 cents per kilometre.

    • If the benefit does not meet all conditions, continue to Step 8b - Calculate using the reasonable allowance rate

    • If the benefit provided to the employee meets all conditions, continue to Step 8a - Calculate using the reduced rate calculation

  8. Calcule the value of the motor vehicle benefit

    You must calculate the benefit related to cost of the employee's personal driving  of the vehicle.

    1. Calculate using the reduced rate calculation

      Use the reduced rate calculation ($0,33 fixed rate for 2023 (or $0.29 for 2022) per kilometre for personal driving) if all conditions in Step 7 are met to calculate the employee's driving of the vehicle.

      Calculations

      • Total number of kilometres for personal driving
      • multiply by $0.33 for 2023 (fixed rate)
      • equals This is the amount for the operating costs benefit
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you for the motor vehicle benefit
      • equals This is the total value of the operating costs benefit
      Calculation example

      In 2022, Matthew was required to use the motor vehicle in connection with his employment in Ontario. The cost of the motor vehicle when it was bought, including options and accessories, and the HST was $50,000. The motor vehicle was made available to Matthew throughout the year (365 days). In verifying his travel, you found that he drove 10,000 km for personal use out of a total of 12,000 km. Matthew reimbursed you $300 for operating expenses 45 days before the end of the year. Matthew meets the conditions to calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      • 10,000 km is the total number of kilometres Matthew personally drove
      • multiply by $0.29 is the fixed rate for 2022
      • equals $2,900 is the amount for the operating costs benefit
      • minus $300 is the amount Matthew reimbursed for the motor vehicle benefit
      • equals $2,600 is the total value of the operating costs benefit
    2. Calculate using the reasonable allowances rates calculation

      Use the reasonable allowance rate  calculation if the conditions in Step 7 are not met to calculate the employee's driving of the vehicle.

      Calculations

      • Total number of kilometres for personal driving
      • multiply by Reasonable allowance rate - First 5,000 kilometres
      • multiply by Reasonable allowance rate - Additional kilometres
      • equals This is the amount for the operating costs benefit
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you for the operating costs benefit
      • equals This is the total value of the operating costs benefit
      Calculation example

      In 2022, Matthew was required to use the motor vehicle in connection with his employment in Ontario. The cost of the motor vehicle when it was bought, including options and accessories, and the HST was $50,000. The automobile was made available to Matthew throughout the year (365 days). In verifying his travel, you found that he drove 10,000 km for personal use out of a total of 12,000 km. Matthew reimbursed you $300 for operating expenses 45 days before the end of the year. Matthew does not meet the conditions to calculate using the fixed rate calculation.

      • 10,000 km is the total number of kilometres Matthew for personal drive
      • multiply by $0.61 is the fixed rate for 2022 on the first 5,000 kilometres
      • multiply by $0.55 is the fixed rate for 2022 on the additional kilometres
      • equals $5,800 ($3,050 + $2,750) is the amount for the operating costs benefit
      • minus $300 is the amount Matthew reimbursed for the operating costs benefit
      • equals $5,500 is the total value of the operating costs benefit

      Employee's allowable employment expenses

      Your employee may be able to claim certain employment expenses on their income tax and benefit return if, under the contract of employment, the employee had to pay for their expenses. This contract of employment does not have to be in writing but you and your employee have to agree to the terms and understand what is expected.

      In this situation, you need to complete Form Form T2200, Declaration of Conditions of Employment and give it to your employee so they can deduct employment expenses from their income. By signing the form, you are only certifying that the employee met the conditions of employment and had to pay for the expenses under their employment contract.

      It is the employee's responsibility to claim the expenses on their income tax and benefits return and to keep records to support the claim.

      Example 1

      You allow your employees to use their personal motor vehicle for business and pay them a monthly motor vehicle allowance to pay for the operating expenses and you include the allowance in the employees' employment income as a taxable benefit

      Example 2

      You have a formal telework arrangement with your employees that allows the employees to work at home. Your employees pays for the expenses of their work space on their own
