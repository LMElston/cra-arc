Educational assistance

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may pay, reimburse or provide an allowance for educational expenses to your employee, a former employee or an employee's family member.

Determine if the benefit is taxable

Generally, if you pay, reimburses or provide an allowance for educational costs to your employee, a former employee or an employee's family member, the benefit is taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

Situations

Situation: You provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your current employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your current employee, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following applies:

  • Course taken to maintain or upgrade your employee's employment related skills or related to business/general employment matters
  • Your employee returns to work for you for a reasonable amount of time (you must be able to justify your position regarding the length of the period of time being reasonable)
  • Training is not for your employee's personal interest

You must be able to clearly demonstrate you are the primary beneficiary of the membership.

Your employee cannot claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit, or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Taxable situation

If the benefit for educational assistance does not meet all of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

Continue to Calculate the value of the benefit.

Situation: You provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your future employee

Non-taxable situation

If you provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your future employee, this is not taxable as an employee benefit because there is no employee/employer relationship between the payer and the future employee (student).

The individual must report the amounts received on their T1 return.

Example

A student may receive educational assistance in exchange for which they agree to work at the end of their studies or training for the person granting the financial assistance.

Report on T4A slip

The payer must report the full amount of the educational assistance paid on a T4A slip. If they meet certain criteria, the employee may claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Situation: You pay the cost for a research grant to a recipient (not your employee) to assist them to carry out research

Non-taxable situation

If you pay the cost for research grant to a recipient to assist them to carry out research, this is not taxable as an employee benefit because there is no employee/employer relationship between the payer and the recipient.

The recipient must report the amounts received on their T1 return.

Report on T4A slip

The payer must report the full amount of the research grant paid on a T4A slip. If they meet certain criteria, the employee may claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit or the educational amount, on their T1 return.
