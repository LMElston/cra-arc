Calculate the GST/HST to remit on employee benefits

You may be looking for:

  • GST/HST rates on benefits
 

How to calculate manually the amount of GST/HST to remit on employee benefits.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    Before you calculate, you need to know if the benefit is taxable.

    Learn more: Determine if a benefit is taxable

    • If the benefit is not taxable, do not continue to next step. You do not need to calculate the GST/HST.
    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to Step 3.

  2. Determine if you need to remit the GST/HST for the benefit

    Generally, you have to remit GST/HST on the benefit related to automobile standby charges and operating expenses.

    GST/HST considered collected or not collected

    Situation: GST/HST not considered collected

    Under the Excise Tax Act, you are not considered to have collected the GST/HST if you make an election by filling out Form GST30, Election for Passenger Vehicles or Aircraft to be Deemed to be Used Exclusively in Non-Commercial Activities (or state in writing the information required on the form) and one of the following conditions apply:

    • You are an individual or a partnership and the vehicle that you have bought is used less than 90% in the commercial activities of the business
    • You are not an individual, a partnership, or a financial institution, and the vehicle that you bought is used 50% or less in the commercial activities of the business
    • You are a financial institution and choose to treat the vehicle you lease or have bought as being used only in non-commercial activities of the business
    • You are not a financial institution and you lease the vehicle which you use 50% or more in non-commercial activities of the business, and you choose to treat it as being used 90% or more in such non-commercial activities

    You do not have to send the Form GST30 to the CRA. You must keep this form in your records in case you are asked for it.

    If all conditions are met, you do not need to do other steps or calculations.

    Continue to Step 15 - Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST.

    Situation 2: GST/HST considered collected

    If the conditions above are not met, the GST/HST is considered to have been collected and needs to be remitted.

    Continue to Step 13 - Determine which GST/HST rate to apply.

  3. Determine which GST/HST rate to apply

    The GST/HST rates to use are based on location. The location means:

    • Last establishment of the registrant at which the employee ordinarily worked, or to which the employee ordinarily reported in the tax year
    • Where the shareholder resides at the end of the tax year

    Large businesses located in Ontario

    When the location is in Ontario, the percentage may be different if the registrant is a large business. In general, a person is considered a large business if the person is a registrant and:

    • the person has a threshold amount for the recapture of input tax credits (RITCs) of more than $10 million in their last fiscal year that ended before the recapture period, or
    • the person is one of the following financial institutions, or a person that is related to one of the following financial institutions: a bank; a credit union; a corporation that is licensed or otherwise authorized under the laws of Canada or a province to carry on in Canada the business of offering to the public its services as a trustee; an insurer or any other person whose principal business is providing insurance under insurance policies; a segregated fund of an insurer, an investment plan, or the person is the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation.

    For more information, see GST/HST Technical Information Bulletin B-104, Harmonized Sales Tax – Temporary Recapture of Input Tax Credits in Ontario and British Columbia.

    If you are, or were, a large business, and the benefits relate to a motor vehicle that was subject to the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable on that vehicle you are considered to have collected, for 2022, the GST/HST as a percentage of the value of the benefits as follows:

    • 4/104, if the recapture rate was 100% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable on that motor vehicle
    • 6/106, if the recapture rate was 75% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Ontario on that motor vehicle
    • 6.5/106.5, if the recapture rate was 75% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Prince Edward Island on that motor vehicle
    • 8/108, if the recapture rate was 50% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Ontario on that motor vehicle
    • 9/109, if the recapture rate was 50% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Prince Edward Island on that motor vehicle
    • 10/110, if the recapture rate was 25% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Ontario on that motor vehicle
    • 11.5/111.5, if the recapture rate was 25% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Prince Edward Island on that motor vehicle
    • 12/112, if the recapture rate was 0% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Ontario on that motor vehicle
    • 14/114, if the recapture rate was 0% on the last day of the last reporting period in which you reported the RITC for the provincial part of the HST paid or payable in Prince Edward Island on that motor vehicle

  4. Calculate the amount of GST/HST to remit on the automobile benefit

    Use the following to calculate the GST/HST on the automobile benefit.

    Calculate the GST/HST to remit

    Standby charge calculations

    • Applicable tax fraction  X amount of the standby charge (from step 9)
    • plus Applicable tax fraction  X amount of the standby charge reimbursement (from step 9)
    • plus Applicable tax fraction  X amount of the payment to third party for personal use (from step 11)
    • equals Amount of GST/HST on the standby charge benefit

    Operating cost calculations

    • Applicable prescribed %   X amount of the operating cost (from step 10)
    • plus Applicable tax fraction  X amount of the operating cost reimbursement (from step 10)
    • equals Amount of GST/HST on the operating benefit

    Total amount to remit calculations

    • Amount of GST/HST on the standby charge benefit
    • plus Amount of GST/HST on the operating benefit
    • equals Amount of GST/HST to remit on the automobile benefit

    If you are considered a large business, the GST/HST calculations may be different. If you cannot claim an input tax credit for the GST/HST you may not have to remit the GST/HST on that benefit.

    These amounts must be remitted in the year after the year you provided the benefit as follows:

    • Employee benefit: at the end of February (this is also the deadline for distributing the slips to your employees)
    • Shareholder benefit: at the end of June
    Calculation example

    In 2022, Matthew was required to use the automobile in connection with his employment in Ontario. The cost of the automobile was $50,000 when it was bought, including options, accessories, and the HST. He was not selling or leasing automobiles. Matthew's employer made the automobile available to him throughout the year (365 days). In verifying his travel, his employer found that he drove 10,000 km for personal use out of a total of 12,000 km. Matthew reimbursed his employer $500 for the standby charge, $300 for operating expenses 45 days before the end of the year and $200 directly to a third party in the year. Matthew did not provide a written notice to use the alternative method of calculating the operating expenses benefit.

    Standby charge calculations

    • $X is the HST calculation for the standby charge (12/112 X $12,000 (from step 9)
    • plus $X is the HST calculation for the standby charge reimbursement ($500 X 12/112 in Ontario) (amount from step 9)
    • plus $X is the HST calculation on the payments to third party for personal use ($166.67 X 12/112 in Ontario) (amount from step 11)
    • equals $X is the HST calculation for the total standby charge benefit

    Operating cost calculations

    • $XXX is the HST calculation for the operating cost expense ($2,900 X 9% in Ontario) (amount from step 10)
    • plus $XX is the HST calculation for the operating costs reimbursement ($300 X 9% in Ontario) (amount from step 10)
    • equals $X is the HST calculation for the total operating benefit

    Total amount to remit calculations

    • $X is the HST calculation for the total standby charge benefit
    • plus $X is the HST calculation for the total operating benefit
    • equals $X is the HST calculation for the amount to remit on the automobile benefit

    Matthew's employer needs to remit $XXX for the HST as he is considered a small business.
