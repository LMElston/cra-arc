Guidance on how to determine the province of employment (POE)

Content has been developed to provide guidance only. The guidance does not replace the new CRA administrative policy and does not replace the law.

Step 1 - Determine the type of income

You need to determine the POE based on the type of income.

What is the type of income?

  • Employment income
  • Non-employment income (for example: pension income, retiring allowances, RRSP and CPP/QPP)

Step 2 - Determine the residency status of the employee or the employer

You need to determine the POE based on the residency status .

What is the residency status?

  • Employee is a resident of Canada
  • Employee is not a resident of Canada for the full year
  • Employer does not have an establishment in Canada but employee works in Canada

Step 3 - Determine how the employee reports

Where the employee is a resident of Canada, you need to consider how the employee reports for work. This must be done based on a thorough review of the facts in each specific case.

Does the employee report for work physically (there is no minimum amount of time the employee has to report to that place) at an establishment of the employer?

  • Yes - Reports physically (in-person) to only 1 establishment
  • Yes - Reports physically (in-person) to more than 1 establishment
  • No - Does not report physically

Employee reports for work physically to only 1 establishment of the employer

The province of employment is where the employee reports for work.

There is no minimum amount of time the employee has to report to that place.

Employee reports for work physically to more than 1 establishment of the employer

The province of employment is the province in which the establishment of the employer is located where the employee had either:

  • Spent the most time
  • Worked last, if the employee reported to each establishment for the same amount of time

Step 4 - Determine if the employee is considered attached to the establishment of the employer

Under the new CRA administrative policy, the employee is considered to be reporting for work at an establishment of the employer if one of the following apply:

  • New CRA administrative policy

    Where a full-time teleworking agreement  was entered into, the employee can be reasonably regarded as attached to the establishment of the employer

  • The employee reports for work physically (there is no minimum amount of time the employee has to report to that place)

Step 4a - Determine if the employee is considered attached to the establishment of the employer

You need to consider in order of importance the criteria to determine if an employee can be reasonably considered to be attached to the establishment of the employer. This must be done based on a thorough review of the facts in each specific case.

Analyze and weigh the 6 criteria to determine which establishment of the employer would apply

Choose the criteria for examples and more information to help you determine which establishment of the employer would apply.

  • Criteria 1: If it was not for a full-time teleworking agreement, the establishment where the employee would physically come to work in person to carry out the functions related to their employment duties
  • Criteria 2: If it was not for a full-time teleworking agreement, the establishment where the employee would physically attend meetings, through any type of communication
  • Criteria 3: If it was not for a full-time teleworking agreement, the establishment where the employee would physically receive work-related material or equipment or associated instructions and assistance
  • Criteria 4: If it was not for a full-time teleworking agreement, the establishment where the employee would physically come in person to receive instructions from their employer regarding their duties, through any type of communication
  • Criteria 5: The establishment that is responsible for the employee, as indicated in the contractual agreements between the employer and the employee
  • Criteria 6: The nature of the duties performed by the employee

Analyze the facts

You need to analyze the facts by organizing and assessing the information you gathered. This process will help you decide and confirm the POE.

You must analyze the answer to each question in order of importance to determine if the employee can be reasonable considered attached to the establishment of the employer.

  • Employee is considered attached
  • Employe is not considered attached

Weigh the facts

This is the most important part of analyzing the information. When reviewing the factors, there may be some facts that support the determination of the POE.

Case-by-case basis

Whether the POE is considered attached or not needs to be analyzed on a case-by-case basis. Each case is unique and requires a thorough review of the criteria. There is no one factor that is determinative.

Step 4b - Confirm if the employee is reasonably considered as attached to a specific establishment of the employer

After reviewing and weighing the criteria in step 4a, can the employee be reasonably considered as attached to a specific establishment of the employer?

  • Yes
  • No

Employee reports for work because they are considered attached to a specific establishment of the employer

The province of employment is where the employee reports for work.

There is no minimum amount of time the employee has to report to that place.

Step 3 - Determine the type of non-resident employee

Where the employee is not a Canadian resident for the whole year, the province of employment will change depending on their specific residency status.

What is the situation of the employee?

  • Non-resident, including a commuter
  • Part-year resident
  • Deemed resident or sojourner

Employee is a non-resident, including a commuter

The province of employment is where employment duties are performed if one of the following situation apply:

  • Employee reports for work at an establishment of the employer in Canada
  • Employee works in Canada, but does not report for work at an establishment of the employer
  • Employee works in Canada, but employer does not have an establishment in Canada

Employee is part-year resident

For the part of the year that the employee is either:

  • A resident of Canada, continue to: Employee is a resident of Canada
  • Not a resident of Canada, continue to: Employee is a non-resident, including a commuter

Employee is a deemed resident or sojourner

The employment is considered in Canada but beyond the limits of any province or territory if one of the following situation apply:

  • Employee reports for work at an establishment of the employer in Canada
  • Employee works in Canada, but does not report for work at an establishment of the employer
  • Employee works in Canada, but employer does not have an establishment in Canada

POE for non-employment income
(for example, pension income, retiring allowances, RRSP and CPP/QPP)

The province of employment is the recipient's province or territory of residence if you pay amounts other than employment income, such as pension income, retiring allowance, or RRSP.
