Processing time

Your SR&ED claim will be processed within 60 days of the date we receive your completed claim and your income tax return for the year.

The CRA requires your income tax return for the year you’re claiming to process your SR&ED claim.

Check the status of your claim

We will contact you if your claim needs further review or is missing information.

If your claim is accepted, you will be notified in your Notice of assessment or reassessment.

If you haven’t heard from the CRA after 60 days of submitting your claim, you can contact us to check your claim status.

Changing your claim after you submit

If your claim is incomplete and the CRA notifies you to provide missing information, you must submit the information before the reporting deadline.

If the reporting deadline has passed, the CRA will not accept any additional information, and the SR&ED expenditures and related ITC will be lost.

If your claim requires a detailed review

Some claims may be selected for further review. This can happen if the CRA was not able to confirm the eligibility of the work based on the information provided in the claim. The CRA may also need further information about the expenditures claimed.

We recommend senior technical and financial personnel be available for the review to provide in depth knowledge of the work, and the company’s finances.

You may be asked to provide supporting records to help reviewers understand the work done, and how expenses associated with the work were determined.

In some cases, The CRA may request an on-site meeting to ask further questions or clarify details.

The review may result in adjustments to your claim. We will provide you with a report outlining the outcome.

For detailed information about the technical review process: The SR&ED Technical Review: A Guide for Claimants

If your claim requires advisory support

If it is your first SR&ED claim or you haven’t filed a claim in the last 3 years, you may be selected for the First-Time Claimant Advisory Service.

This mandatory service provides you with recommendations and guidance to support your future claims.

Disagreeing with our decision

If you disagree with the CRA’s decision on your SR&ED claim or claim review, you still have options.

You have the right to object the CRA’s decision. You have up to 90 days from the date of your Notice of assessment or reassessment to file an objection.

For more information about filing an objection: Resolving disputes

If you don’t agree with the CRA’s decision on your objection, you may then file an appeal.

For more information about the objection and appeals processes: Objections and appeal rights under the Income Tax Act
