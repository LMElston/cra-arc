Report expenditures

How to report your expenditures

To report your expenditures, you will need to calculate how much you spent on your SR&ED project during the tax year.

To report your SR&ED expenditures, you must:

  1. Calculate your allowable expenditures

    These represent expenditures you incurred in relation to SR&ED work done during the tax year. They include salary or wages, materials, contracts, overhead, and third-party payments.

  2. Calculate your pool of deductible expenditures

    This is an adjusted amount, based on your allowable expenditures, that represents the amount available to be deducted against your net income.

  3. Calculate your qualified SR&ED expenditures

    This represents your expenditures that qualify for an investment tax credit (ITC).

Use the SR&ED expenditures claim form to report your expenditures:
Form T661, Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) Expenditures Claim

If you’re a member of a partnership

When SR&ED is done by a partnership, some special requirements may apply:

  • A partnership’s SR&ED expenditures must be deducted in the calculation of the partnership’s net income or loss in the tax year they are incurred
  • A partnership will not have a pool of SR&ED expenditures to carry forward to future tax years
  • Members of partnership cannot include the partnership’s SR&ED expenditures in their pool of deductible SR&ED expenditures

SR&ED expenditures are deducted at the partnership level and the partnership should report its expenditures using Form T661. A member of the partnership may do this on the partnership’s behalf.

Partners do not include any of the partnership’s SR&ED expenditures if reporting SR&ED expenditures on their own on Form T661.

Review the guide for more information on how partnerships must fill out T661:
T4088 Guide, Partnerships
