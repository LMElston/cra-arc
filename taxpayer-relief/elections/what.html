What are tax elections

In certain circumstances, a taxpayer can choose between:

  • using a default set of tax rules
  • electing (choosing) an alternative set of rules

This "election" gives taxpayers options and flexibility in handling their financial matters.

Limitations

A request to have a late or amended election accepted, or to revoke an election, is limited to the provisions of the Income Tax Act.

Most election provisions do not:

  • permit you to file an election once the due date for making the election is missed
  • give you the ability to modify or cancel an original election that was filed on time

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) may, however, allow you to modify or cancel elections that have already been filed, or file an election even if you missed the deadline.

  • Amending your election: amending your election is best done before the date the election is due. You may apply to have your election amended after the due date, but the CRA may not accept your request.
  • Revoking your election: revoking your election is best done before the date the election is due. You may apply to have your election revoked after the due date, but the CRA may not accept your request.

Note: you may be liable to a penalty if you file your election late or apply to amend or revoke your election after the due date. For more information, see Penalty for accepting a late, amended, or revoked election.
